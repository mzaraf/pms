Performance Management System (PMS) is a comprehensive process used by organizations to monitor and evaluate employees' work performance, set goals, provide feedback, and make decisions about promotions, rewards, and other aspects related to employee development and productivity. The primary objectives of a performance management system are to align individual goals with organizational goals, identify areas for improvement, recognize achievements, and support career development.
Goal Setting: Establishing clear, specific, and measurable objectives for individual employees or teams that align with the organization's strategic goals.
Performance Appraisal: Conducting periodic assessments of employees' performance against established goals and competencies. This can involve self-assessment, peer reviews, and evaluations by managers.
Recognition and Rewards: Recognizing and rewarding employees for their achievements, contributions, and exceptional performance through various means such as bonuses, promotions, awards, or public acknowledgment.
Training and Development: Providing training, resources, and opportunities for skill enhancement and career advancement to support employees in meeting their performance goals and organizational needs.
Performance Improvement Plans (PIPs): Implementing structured plans for employees who are not meeting performance expectations, outlining specific actions, timelines, and support mechanisms to help them improve.
